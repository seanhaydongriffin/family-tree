Fergus Alistair Allan - Letters

 

AUS 401265 A.C. II Allan F.A.

No 4 I.T.S.

(Mt Breacon)

VICTOR HARBOUR

SOUTH AUSTRALIA

 

7th January 1941

 

Dear Mum and Dad

 

This is the first chance I've had to write.  We arrived yesterday for lunch and have been going flat out since.

 

I've got all my uniform, including socks, braces, razor, soap, 3 brushes, comb, toothbrush and everything.  The quarters are very comfortable and the meals excellent - more than one can eat.

 

The only leave we get is Wednesday night and from Saturday noon to Sunday night so it doesn't look as if I can get home until the eight weeks are up.

 

Well there's not much I can say, as we are not permitted to divulge any information about the force except that from what I've seen up to date it's a good life although at first it's a bit hard to get used to the discipline.

 

On the other side I've set out how to address letters and they only need a 1d stamp.  I am writing this in bed and I think I'll go to sleep now.

 

                                    Your loving son

                                    Fergus.
