Fergus Alistair Allan - Letters

 

AUS 401265

L.A.C.  Allan F.A.

No 2   B.A.G.S (Bombing and Gunnery School)

 RAAF.

Port Pirie

SOUTH AUSTRALIA

9th July 1941

Dear Mother

 

Your letter of 1st instant to hand this morning.  I think my last letter must have gone astray or else been delayed.  I wrote about two weeks ago and have been awaiting a reply since.

 

You will see by the above address that I have shifted. Two weeks ago today we left Mt. Gambier and came straight through here via Adelaide spending a couple of days there.  You will probably have received my last letter by the time this one reaches you - I see your letter went to Mt Gambier and has been delayed a week.  Well I think I mentioned that it was very dusty here, but since then it has rained - it is still raining, and the station is just one big quagmire.

 

Flying has been cancelled due to the 'drome being too sticky. You can't imagine the amount of mud, there is not one blade of grass around the huts - nothing but that sticky red mud, churned up by lorries we and men and the builder still digging trenches all over the place.  Even the tractors are bogged.

 

The weather generally is much warmer than Melbourne and usually one can go out at night without a great-coat, although the last couple of days have reminded me of Melbourne.

 

As things stand at present we will be leaving Port Pirie in a couple of weeks to do one month of astro-navigation at Mount gambier, commencing there on 28th July.  Whether I will get enough time to get back to Melbourne between courses remains to be seen, but I am still hoping.  As a matter of fact Morris Coath is getting married then and he wants me to attend the wedding.

 

We have been doing a bit of air to ground gunnery, that is shooting at targets on the ground whilst flying past at about 150 mph.  Twice I held the record, but twice someone else pipped mu score.  At one time I was Australasia champion air to ground but now I am only second.  I was going up today to try and bust it open but due to the weather we are stuck on the ground.

 

There is not much to write about really and it takes a long time to think up enough to put in a letter.  All we do is work in the day time and sleep at night.  We sleep on the floor on straw palliaces (?) and only today was the ridging put on the roof.   Previously when it rained we had a small river in the centre of the hut and we had to turn our bed on an angle to keep our toes out of then water.

 

So Margaret and Gordon have shifted to Mentone.  It will be nice for the kiddies in the summer time.  By your description it seems a very nice place.

 

I haven't received Ron's letter to date but perhaps it has gone to Mt. Gambier too, so I will wait till I get it before writing to him I answered one of Don's letters the same day as I sent yours.

 

Well how are you keeping and how is Dad? You didn't mention in your letter.  I am doing fine; I had a bit of a cold a couple of weeks ago, which I have got rid of.  The food on the station is one of the redeeming features and every one is satisfied.

 

We have cereals or porridge with hot or cold milk, eggs and bacon or sometimes lamb's fry for breakfast.  Then often we have a 3 course meal for both dinner and tea. It is well cooked and plenty of it.  Then we can have as much bread and jam or honey as we like.  We usually get fruit once a day, but we can buy beautiful big oranges at the canteen for 1d each. So I can eat about 4 or 5 f those as well.

 

But no matter how much I eat I don't think I will ever get any fatter.

 

I was flying last Sunday but went to church in the evening.  On the whole they are a different class of people than at Mt Gambier - instead of the rich squatter we have the ordinary working man, mostly employed by the smelters, which is said to be the biggest lead and silver smelting works in the world.

 

After church we went around to the "Thumbs Up" club which is just new and opened by the town's people for the benefit of men in uniform.  There we can play table tennis, billiards, drafts etc, or just sit down and read. And at supper time we can get nice sandwiches and scones with coffee for 4d.

 

If the weather clears up, as it looks like doing at present, I think I will go to the pictures tonight - we usually go about twice a week as the lights are not good enough at present for studying at night.

 

I will finish now or else I won't have anything to write about next time.

 

Trusting you are both well and give my love to all the family

 

I am

                        Your loving son

                                                            Fergus A.
