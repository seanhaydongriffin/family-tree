Fergus Alistair Allan - Letters

 

AUS 401265 A.C. II Allan F.A.

No 4 I.T.S.

(Mt Breacon)

VICTOR HARBOUR

SOUTH AUSTRALIA

 

17.01.41

Dear Mother

 

I don't seem to get much time to write as often as I like but tonight is leave night and I've got a while to spare.  Tomorrow we have a Maths test.  In the preliminary test I got 100% fortunately. We had two inoculations last week but these don't affect one much, and today we were vaccinated. I believe they play up after a few days, so I've just got to await that pleasure.

I hope Dad can get that job at Fisherman's Bend and I wish him the best of luck.

 

Last week I went to Adelaide with a few of the other chaps.  The people in Adelaide treated us well. It cost us nothing on the train (nearly 8 hrs there and back) and we went to the "Cheer Up Hut" and got free meals, also they arranged for some people with cars to take us for rides into the hills. They were extra nice and drove us to their weekend place for afternoon tea.

 

I did not have time to visit any of Margaret's friends in Adelaide and besides we all kept together, and went to the pictures Saturday night and for a row on the river on Sunday morning.

 

Yes they gave me a thick pullover with my kit. Long sleeves and very warm but the weather here is seasonable, not like Melbourne weather.

 

We get plenty of exercise here; besides all our various studies we have to do 1 to 2 hrs of drill, 1 hr of physical training and 1 hour of swimming each day and on Wednesday afternoons, organised sopor.

 

If you would like to know the menu to be satisfied that I am eating plenty, and believe me we need it, this is a sample for a day.

 

            Breakfast -       Plate of Weeties with warm milk or porridge

                                    2 eggs and bacon  or lamb's fry

                                    tea or coffee

                                    as much bread and jam (with butter) as we can eat.

            Dinner- Fried fish and potatoes or cold meat and salad

                                    Pudding

                                    Fruit occasionally

                                    Tea and bread etc

            Tea -                Soup

                                    Hot or cold meat (either roast beef or mutton with spuds and vegetables)

                                    Dessert

                                    Tea and bread etc.

 

So you can see they don't starve us.  There is actually an allowance of 1/9d per day each for messing.  I believe it is only 1/3d in the army.  Every meal time an officer comes around and calls out for any complaints.

 

I will get my photo taken as soon as I can. And about my bank account I don't think it's necessary to advise them, as Drewett St is still my permanent address.

 

I had to buy a big case to put all my excess clothes in so I think I will have to send my civilian cloths home later on.  I will advise you before sending.

 

Well there is not much else I can say at present and it is nearly lights out so I will finish off.

Excuse the pencil but I'm in bed and I can write faster with pencil.

 

I must write to Ron, but if you see him before I write to him, wish him all the best for me and tell him I miss him, especially at weekends.

 

Give my love to all the family and all the children and I hope to see you all in about another 6 weeks.  There's no chance of getting over before then.

 

                                    Yours lovingly

                                                Fergus
