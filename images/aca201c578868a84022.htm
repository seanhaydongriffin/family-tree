Fergus Alistair Allan - Letters

 

AUS 401265 A.C. II Allan F.A.

No 4 I.T.S.

(Mt Breacon)

VICTOR HARBOUR

SOUTH AUSTRALIA

 

25th January 1941

 

Dear Mother and Dad

 

Well we are getting some Melbourne weather here.  It started raining Thursday night and hasn't stopped since.  The grounds where we do our physical training is three feet under water, but our parade ground is just mud so we can't drill. We do Morse Code instead. The railway line has been washed away between Victor Harbour and Adelaide.

 

Last Wednesday afternoon it was terrifically hot and we were having sport on the golf links about half a mile away when the fire alarm siren rang at the camp.  We had to drop everything and run for our lives up hill and when we got there found it was just a little joke to see how quickly we would answer. It is an order that as soon as we hear the siren we have to run to the parade ground n matter where we are, even down in the town, and believe me it is a loud siren.

 

Thanks for the photos; Ron wrote to me before he went to Seymour.  About my motor cycle licence, I don't want it to let it run out because I will want to use it if I come home on leave, so would you send a 5/- postal note with the licence to Motor Reg. Branch.  There is no need to tell them my address as my private address is still Drewett St. The post office here is never open when we get leave so I can't get one myself.

 

I got 96% in the Maths test last week and 100% today and the Commanding Officer had me paraded before him and he asked me how I was liking the place, and was I happy etc. and had quite a little chat. He did this to only one other chap in the flight, a chap named Jones, and a personal friend of mine.  We went to church together last Sunday night and going again tomorrow if it clears up a bit (n.b. with Jones, not the CO).

 

There were three chaps in our flight who failed in their test, two who were made gunners, and the other, who was named Seppelt (son of Seppelt of Seppelt Wines) was dismissed, so you can see they keep you up to the mark.

 

It is a terrible day today, strong winds and driving rain, and looking out of the door there is ahuge sea rolling in and crashing over the breakwater. Looking out of the window the Hindmarsh River is in flood and where it enters the sea the water is muddy and tossing about.

 

The other day we all had our photos taken for record purposes and I am enclosing a copy of one.  It is not a good photo, but it cost nothing. We also had one taken of the flight; these cost 3/- each so I am getting one.

 

It is much cooler out in the huts but we have five blankets each.  I only use 4 and one is for a pillow with the blue kit bag they gave us.

 

We get a variety of jobs here that it is impossible to become monotonous.  We have to make our beds, fold our blankets in the correct manner, clean our boots, sweep and dust the hut as well as clean the window.  On Monday night it is what we call panic night.  All floors have to be scrubbed, all windows cleaned, door handles polished, electric light shades cleaned and a Host of tother things.  Then apart from studying Maths, Morse code, Air Force Law, armaments, gas, medical, drill, physical training and swimming etc, we get what s called fatigue.  That is, have to clean up the garden and dozens of other jobs.

 

My vaccination did not break out and up to date have not affected me.  The medical officer inspected our arms yesterday and seems satisfied.

 

We are going to the pictures tonight, "The Beachcomber", with Chas Laughton, is on.   They get pictures here about 6 months after Melbourne, but I think I can see it twice.

 

I will finish now as I've got a law book with 984 pages that I want to study.

 

Trusting you is both well and looking after yourselves.

 

                        Your loving son

                                    Fergus.
